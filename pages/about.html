Information about Chuanlong (Ben) Du

My LinkedIn | My Facebook | My GitHub

My name is Chuanlong (Ben) Du. I got my Bachelor's degree in statistics from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) and currently a PhD student in the Department of Statistics in Iowa State University (ISU). Stephen Vardeman is my academic advisor and Dan Nordman is coadvisor. My researches with Stephen Vardeman and Dan Nordman mostly focus on Bayesian analysis and machine learning. One of my researches is about modeling equivalence classes of 3-D orientations resulting from crystalline symmetry. Another related one is about clustering equivalence classes of 3-D orientations. Besides researches for my disseration, I also work as a research assistant for Dan Nettleton on biostatistics stuff. I did lots of project with Dr. Nettleton. I worked on 2-color microarray data, AffyMetrics GeneChip data and most recently RNAseq data. I used linear models, generalized linear models, mixed linear models, etc in theses projects.

I'm a sports lover. My favorate sports are Badminton and Pingpong. I also go bowling quite often. I play tennis and golf occasionally. I watch football, baseball and NBA. In my free time, I also like to learning computer skills a lot. I was a Windows user and did lots of programming in VB before. For example, I worte an email system in VB.net to analyze my emails before. And before Dropbox became so popular, I wrote a backup application in VB.net to synchronize my files across my laptops. Now I work mostly in Linux. I learned lots of programming languges by myself such as C/C++, Java, Python, Ruby, etc. I use mainly R and SAS in my research, however, I do use other languages for high performance computing. I used to use Java a lot in my research (mainly for parallel computing), but I switched to C++ later due to the appearance of Rcpp.
I do not use Python and Ruby very often, however, I force myself to write code in Python and Ruby sometimes. Because I don't like to get trapped in one programming language. I'd like to make myself open-minded and always have the most suitable tool for my work. I wrote a small program in Python to help me manage my posts on this blog. I also wrote a small program in Ruby to automatically extract bill information from my phone statement, calculate bill for each member, and then send email to notify everyone to pay bill on time.

All in all, I have strong statistics background and good computer skills. I'm a motivated person with greate enthusiasm. If you want to learn more about my professional achievements, please check my resume here.
